### 4. Problem Exploration Narrative
Giorgi’s interview illustrates the **Notification Noise Paradox**. Because Messenger groups are used for both logistics (important) and social banter (unimportant), users like Giorgi are forced to mute them to stay focused on studies. 

This directly led to a failure: his team played a man down and lost, leaving Giorgi with significant "social guilt." He articulated a need for a **calendar-based mental model**. He doesn't want a "conversation"; he wants a "deadline notification." His current workaround—relying on a "buddy" to DM him—is a high-risk strategy that fails whenever the buddy is also distracted.

### 5. Mom Test Compliance Check
* **Pass:** I asked Giorgi to show me the specific Messenger group that he had muted. Seeing the sheer volume of "noise" (memes/voice notes) confirmed why he had to mute it. This provided objective proof of the problem.
* **Fail:** I caught myself asking, "Would a calendar-style notification be better for you?" This is a leading question. 
* **Correction:** I should have asked: "How do you usually handle reminders for things you can't afford to miss, like exams?" to see what systems he already trusts and uses.

### 6. Personal Reflection
Giorgi made me realize that the "social" aspect of group chats is actually the **enemy of logistics**. By mixing chat with schedules, we are guaranteeing that people will miss updates. This reinforced the idea that our solution must be "silent" until it is "essential." It’s about quality of notification, not quantity.
